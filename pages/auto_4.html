Buying a Used Car? Look Out for These Things!

  • Automotive

Congratulations! If you are reading this, you have decided to buy a used car instead of a new vehicle. The path you’ve chosen is a great way of getting a car without burning a hole in your pocket. But this process can be quite daunting for some. That is why, we have curated a checklist of everything you should look for while shopping for a used car:

Vehicle History

Get your hands on as much information on the vehicle’s history as you can. Speak to the current owner, run the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) through services like CARFAX to see if has gotten into any major accidents.

Insect the Exteriors

Inspect the car’s exteriors thoroughly and keep an eye out for rust or paint damage. If there are spots with serious rusting, you might want to look for a different car. To make sure there are no problems with the car’s frame, see if its wheels are level on the ground and if the hood & trunk close seamlessly.

Check the Tire Condition

Check to see if the tire treads are worn out evenly on all four tires. Why? Uneven wear suggests poor wheel alignment.

Mileage

Calculate the car’s mileage by dividing the miles on the odometer by the vehicle’s age. A car with higher mileage can have a more worn out engine.

Inspect the Interior

Do a quick interior check to make sure the electronics are functional and the upholstery doesn’t need to be changed. Tinker around with the radio system to check the speaker volume, turn on the air conditioner and heat to see if they work properly. Watch out for any stains, tears or cracks on the upholstery fabric and seat belts.

Test Drive

This step is the most important part of buying any vehicle, not just a used one. Chalk out a test drive route so you can drive on various terrains like a congested road, the highway and a bumpy road. Test its acceleration, braking, maneuverability and suspension.

Bring in An Expert

If you think you want to go ahead and buy this car, bring in a mechanic for a proper inspection of the entire vehicle. This isn’t a cheap process but will save you from buying a car that could be a potential threat to you and others on the road.
