How To Start Your Savings Journey

  • Finance

An emergency fund is the bedrock of financial security. It brings you a sense of comfort and confidence – knowing you can weather unexpected hardships without raiding your retirement funds. That said, saving money is not easy. It requires you to push back against cultural conditioning and forgo immediate pleasures for the sake of (possible) future calamities.

Here are our tips to successfully develop a savings habit:

Keep Your Savings out Of Reach

Set up an external saving account at a bank that’s different from the one you have a checking account at. This will erect the necessary barriers between you and your reserve cash. Plus,  a separate high-yield savings account may also offer you better interest.

Having to jump through hoops to spend your savings is likely to dissuade you from doing so – and you’ll avoid buyer’s remorse, as an added bonus.

Don’t Set Yourself up To Fail

So many of us resolve to save money each new year and then fail to do so. There’s a reason for that – setting lofty goals.

Instead, focus on the process. Tell yourself that you’ll only do it for three months. That way, the sacrifice won’t feel so great, and the commitment less permanent. Plus, you’re more likely to succeed if you decide to save $100 from each paycheck than $10,000 in the next year.

That success will motivate you to keep going and by the time the three months are up, you won’t want to stop.

Take Advantage of Windfalls

It’s gonna take a while to build up your emergency fund if you’re setting aside $50 from each paycheck. However, a nice year-end bonus or birthday money can help you make major strides.

By saving (all or most of) that money, you get closer to that moment where you’ve got enough saved up to be invested.

Nothing will get you more stoked about savings than seeing those numbers go up. Once you get there and experience how good financial security feels, saving will become a lot easier.
