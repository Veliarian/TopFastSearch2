Follow These Tips While You Exercise If You Have Diabetes

  • Health

If you have diabetes, exercise has to be on your to-do list. Listed below are some go-to tips that will help you get started:

Make a List of Fun Activities

You don’t have to force yourself to go to a boring gym, there are so many other options to choose from. Think about something you enjoyed in the past or something you’ve always wanted to try. Anything that raises your heart rate works: Dancing, yoga, walking, swimming, and sports are a few suggestions. 

If you’re in good health aside from diabetes, scuba-diving, rock-climbing or other adventure sports would be a good option too. However, make sure that you get the right training and aren’t performing these activities alone. Always carry some fast-acting carbs like glucose tablets or a sports gel with you. 

Get Your Doctor’s Permission

Inform your doctor what you want to do, so that they can ensure that you’re ready for these activities. They might also need to see if you need to change your insulin, diabetes medicines, or meals. Your doctor would also be the best person to suggest if the time of the day you exercise matters. 

Check Your Blood Sugar

If you choose to exercise for more than an hour, regularly monitor your blood sugar levels so you’ll know when you need a snack. It is also advised to check your blood sugar levels after every workout. 

Carry Carbs

Since workouts might lower your blood sugar, make sure that you keep a small carbohydrate snack handy. You can carry a fruit or a fruit drink, in case your blood sugar gets low. 

Ease Into It

Start with 10 minutes of exercise if you’re not an active person. Work it up to about 30 minutes every day gradually. If you feel weak, confused, shaky, or anxious, stop exercising. You should also stop exercising if your heart is racing, you have a headache or you sweat more than usual.
